<p><p>The optical transport equipment market comprises two primary types: short-distance and long-distance fiber optics. Short-distance fiber optics are designed for data transmission over limited spans, typically within buildings or local networks, offering high-speed connectivity with lower latency. In contrast, long-distance fiber optics facilitate communication over extensive distances, connecting cities or regions, and are essential for backbone networks. Both types serve critical roles in enhancing bandwidth, supporting high-capacity data transfer, and meeting the increasing demand for reliable communications infrastructure.</p></p>
