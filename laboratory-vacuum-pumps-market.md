<p><p>The laboratory vacuum pumps market primarily includes dry vacuum pumps and rotary vane vacuum pumps. Dry vacuum pumps utilize a mechanism that eliminates the need for lubricants, making them ideal for clean applications and reducing contamination risks. In contrast, rotary vane vacuum pumps operate through rotating vanes that create a vacuum in a chamber, offering reliable performance for various laboratory processes. Both types serve essential roles in providing controlled environments for experiments, material handling, and sample preservation.</p></p>
