Set-style yogurt is characterized by its thicker texture, as it is fermented directly in containers, resulting in a creamy consistency. In contrast, Swiss-style yogurt is typically creamier and often incorporates fruit or flavoring, being stirred before packaging to achieve a smooth, uniform consistency. Both styles cater to evolving consumer preferences for healthier, protein-rich dairy alternatives and showcase the versatility of goat milk.</p></p>
