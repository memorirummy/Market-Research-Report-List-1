<p><p>Palladium nanoparticles are ultrafine particles of palladium, typically ranging from 1 to 100 nanometers in size. These nanoparticles exhibit unique physical, chemical, and catalytic properties due to their high surface area-to-volume ratio, making them highly valuable in various applications. They are widely used in catalysis, electronics, hydrogen storage, sensors, and medical applications, including drug delivery and cancer treatment. Their exceptional catalytic activity, particularly in hydrogenation and dehydrogenation reactions, has driven their demand in industrial processes.</p><p>The Palladium Nanoparticle Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 12.9% during the forecast period.
