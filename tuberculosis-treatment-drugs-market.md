<p><p>The tuberculosis (TB) treatment drugs market is categorized into two main types: Active TB and Latent TB. Active TB drugs target individuals exhibiting symptoms and needing immediate intervention, often involving a combination of antibiotics over a prolonged period. In contrast, Latent TB drugs treat asymptomatic individuals who harbor the bacteria but do not exhibit active disease, primarily using shorter, less intensive regimens. This distinction is crucial for tailoring effective treatment strategies and addressing the varying needs of TB patients.</p></p>
