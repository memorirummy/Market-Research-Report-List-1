Halothane, though less commonly used now, was historically significant for general anesthesia. Sevoflurane is known for its smooth induction and is frequently preferred in pediatric anesthesia. Atropine, while not an anesthetic itself, is used to reduce secretions and prevent bradycardia during procedures. Together, these agents cater to varied anesthetic needs in clinical practice.</p></p>
