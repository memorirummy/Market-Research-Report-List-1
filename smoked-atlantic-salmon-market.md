<p><p>The Smoked Atlantic Salmon market is categorized into hot smoking and cold smoking. Hot smoking involves curing the salmon at higher temperatures, resulting in a cooked texture and a distinctive smoky flavor, making it ideal for ready-to-eat dishes. Cold smoking, on the other hand, uses lower temperatures, preserving the fish's raw texture and allowing for delicate flavor profiles, typically served on bagels or in salads. Both methods cater to diverse consumer preferences and culinary applications, driving market growth.</p></p>
