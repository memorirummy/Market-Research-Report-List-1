<p><p>The cellulose market comprises two primary types: natural and synthetic cellulose. Natural cellulose, derived from plant sources such as wood and cotton, is widely used in industries like textiles, food, and pharmaceuticals for its biodegradability and biocompatibility. In contrast, synthetic cellulose is chemically engineered to enhance specific properties, including strength and resistance to moisture. This type is often utilized in applications like packaging materials and advanced composites. Both types cater to diverse industrial needs, reflecting a growing demand for sustainable and innovative materials.</p></p>
