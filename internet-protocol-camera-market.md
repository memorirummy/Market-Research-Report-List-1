<p><ul><li>Centralized Internet Protocol Camera</li><li>Decentralized Internet Protocol Camera</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The Internet Protocol (IP) camera market is primarily divided into two types: centralized and decentralized IP cameras. Centralized IP cameras rely on a central server for video storage and processing, offering easier management and control of multiple cameras, making them suitable for larger installations. In contrast, decentralized IP cameras, also known as edge cameras, process and store video data locally, reducing bandwidth usage and enhancing security. This allows for greater flexibility and scalability, particularly in smaller or distributed environments.</p></p>
